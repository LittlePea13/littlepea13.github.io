abstract: Relation Extraction (RE) is a task that identifies relationships between
  entities in a text, enabling the acquisition of relational facts and bridging the
  gap between natural language and structured knowledge. However, current RE models
  often rely on small datasets with low coverage of relation types, particularly when
  working with languages other than English.In this paper, we address the above issue
  and provide two new resources that enable the training and evaluation of multilingual
  RE systems. First, we present SRED$^textrmFM$, an automatically annotated dataset
  covering 18 languages, 400 relation types, 13 entity types, totaling more than 40
  million triplet instances. Second, we propose RED$^textrmFM$, a smaller, human-revised
  dataset for seven languages that allows for the evaluation of multilingual RE systems.
  To demonstrate the utility of these novel datasets, we experiment with the first
  end-to-end multilingual RE model, mREBEL, that extracts triplets, including entity
  types, in multiple languages. We release our resources and model checkpoints at
  [rﾌə́https://www.github.com/babelscape/rebel](lﾌɔ́ttps://www.github.com/babelscape/rebel).
